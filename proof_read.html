Systematic Investment Plan

Systematic Investment Plan is also known as SIP. It’s an investment apparatus to invest in Mutual F unds every month. A fixed amount of money is deducted from your bank account and is invested in a desired mutual fund scheme.

Mutual Funds are bought by values known as Net Asset Value (NAV’s). In a bear market the NAV of the mutual fund scheme will come down hence you can purchase more units of the Mutual Fund scheme. In a bull market the purchased unit will be relatively less in quantity. 

Every Mutual Fund cycle generates returns. Every long term investor is the winner here.

Portfolio Hedging

Hedging refers to the procedure of insuring a trade or a portfolio to prevent it from risk of price action. 

Let’s Understand this with the help of an example Investor A has 100 shares of Infosys, 100 shares of Reliance Industries, 100 shares of ITC, 100 shares of Maruti Suzuki. 
The risk here for the investor is he holds a bullish position in those scripts which might not favour him in the future. In case of any major downside in the scripts he hold he will face a loss. To protect this downside risk Investors, Mutual Fund houses and Hedge Funds Use something called as Hedging In case of this example, Investor A is bullish on some of the heavy weights of The Nifty50 Index.

To protect his portfolio or to insure his portfolio he will hold a subsequent short position in some other form. Portfolio Hedging can be done in 2 ways One by having a short position in the Futures and Options Market Second is by having a long position in GoldIn both the cases all the positions work inversely i.e. If one is a bullish position other one is a bearish position and vice versa. But the question stands here is how many options should I have to hedge to my portfolio.
